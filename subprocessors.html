Sub-Processors

Third-party service providers that process customer data

Last Updated: January 19, 2025

Overview

Fluent Bot uses carefully selected third-party sub-processors to provide our services. This page lists all sub-processors that may access or process customer data, including the type of data processed and the purpose of processing.

We are committed to:

  • Using only reputable, security-conscious sub-processors
  • Ensuring all sub-processors meet appropriate data protection standards
  • Maintaining transparency about how your data is processed
  • Notifying customers of changes to our sub-processor list

Security Measures

All sub-processors are required to maintain appropriate security measures:

  • Encryption: All data transmitted to sub-processors uses TLS/SSL encryption in transit
  • Access Controls: Sub-processors implement role-based access controls and authentication
  • Compliance: All infrastructure sub-processors maintain relevant certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001)
  • Data Minimization: We only transmit data necessary for each sub-processor's specific function

Changes to Sub-Processors

Notification Policy:

We will update this page and notify users via email (if available) or Slack notification at least 30 days before:

  • Adding new sub-processors that will process customer data
  • Making material changes to how existing sub-processors handle data

The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page indicates the most recent change.

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Object: If you object to the use of a specific sub-processor, you may stop using the Service or contact us to discuss alternatives
  • Access: Request information about how sub-processors handle your data
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your data from our systems (sub-processors have their own retention policies)

Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

For enterprise customers or organizations requiring a formal Data Processing Addendum, please contact us at k19tech@gmail.com.

International Data Transfers

All sub-processors currently process data within the United States. For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland:

  • Data transfers to the United States are based on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or adequacy decisions
  • Sub-processors implement appropriate safeguards for international data transfers
  • Users can request more information about transfer mechanisms by contacting k19tech@gmail.com
